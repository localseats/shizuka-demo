概念 — Concept

For the traveler who would rather hear the temple bell at dawn than the elevator chime.

Shizuka is a single machiya house on a quiet lane in Higashiyama. Not a hotel. Not a chain. Just a 120-year-old wooden home, kept as it was, opened for those who want to stay where Kyoto used to live.

家 — The House

Restored,
not renovated.

Built in the early Meiji era, this machiya was the home of a textile merchant. When we found it, it had been empty for fourteen years. The beams were dark with age. The garden had returned to the earth.

We did not change it. We cleaned it, fixed what needed fixing, and left the rest alone. The walls still hold the smoke of a century of winters. The floorboards still creak the way they used to.

一日 — A Day at Shizuka

A day moves slowly here. There is nothing to do, and that is the point.

06:00
朝 — Morning

Coffee in the courtyard

The garden is cold at six. Steam rises from the cup. A neighbour sweeps her doorstep. The temple bell sounds twice from somewhere west.

14:00
昼 — Afternoon

A walk to Gion

Twelve minutes through narrow lanes. Past the wood-fronted shops, the small shrine, the noodle counter that opens only at lunch. Kyoto rewards those who walk slowly.

20:00
夜 — Evening

The cedar bath

The bath is heated by gas now, but the tub is two hundred years old. The wood smells of forest. The window opens to the courtyard. The night is yours.

部屋 — The Rooms

Two tatami rooms.
One bath. One garden.

— The whole house is yours. Each room opens onto the inner courtyard. Futons are laid out at night by a member of our small team and put away in the morning. There is no television. There is no Wi-Fi in the tatami room — only by the kitchen, where you can sit at the wooden counter if you must.

We do not modernise Kyoto. We let our guests slip into it.

— The owner's note
町 — The Neighborhood

Higashiyama,
on foot.

Higashiyama is the Kyoto most travellers come to see, but the lane where Shizuka stands is not on most maps. It is residential. Old. Quiet.

From the front door, almost every famous temple is within thirty minutes on foot. So is the river, the market, and the best soba shop in the city — which has no sign, no website, and no English menu.
